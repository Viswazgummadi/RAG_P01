<<HEADING level=3>>1. SCOPE<<HEADING_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>1.1  This specification lays down the requirements for the materials, fabrica-
tion, erection and supply of rectangular
pressed steel tanks used for the
storage of cold and hot water and certain other liquids under pressure not
greater than the static head corresponding to the depth of the tank.<<PARAGRAPH_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>1.2 This specification
does not cover the requirements
of tanks storing
liquids having temperature
higher than lOO”C, or those tanks subject to
earth or other external pressures besides wind pressure.<<PARAGRAPH_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>2.  TERMINOLOGY<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>2.0  For the purpose of this specification, the following definitions shall apply.<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>2.1  Cold Water or Liquid -
Water or liquid at a temperature not exceed-
ing 40°C.<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>2.2  Hot Water or Liquid -
Water or liquid at a temperature
above
4O”C, but not exceeding
100°C.<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>3. TYPES<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>3.1  Pressed steel tanks are of the following three types:<<HEADING_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>Type 1 Tanks with all flanges external ( see Fig. 1 )
Type 2 Tanks with all flanges internal<<PARAGRAPH_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>Type 3 Tanks with bottom flanges internal and side flanges external<<PARAGRAPH_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>3.1.1
Each of the above types may be either with open top or with covered
top.<<PARAGRAPH_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>4.  GENERAL<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>4.1  Pressed steel tanks are not recommended for depths greater than 5 m.<<HEADING_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>4.2 Type 1 tanks are normally used where a plain internal surface is necessary
or where there are no restrictions as to external access or where the exterior
of the tank is to be lagged.<<PARAGRAPH_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>4.3 Type 2 tanks are normally used at a location where access to the exterior
for erection is precluded due to insufficient space inside a building.<<PARAGRAPH_END>>

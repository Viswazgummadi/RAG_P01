<<HEADING level=3>>0. FOREWORD<<HEADING_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>0.1  This
Indian
Standard
( Part
9 )
was
adopted
by
the
Indian
Standards
Institution
on 15 February
1987, after the draft
finalized
by
the
Measurement
of
Works
of
River
Valley
Projects
Sectional
Committee
had
been
approved
by
the
Civil
Engineering
Division
Council.<<PARAGRAPH_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>0.2
In measurement
of quantities
in construction
of river valley projects,
a large diversity
of methods
exist at present
according
to local practices.
This
lack
of uniformity
creates
complication
regarding
measurements
and payments.
This standard
is intended
to provide
guidance
regard-
ing
a uniform
basis
for measurement
of lining
of canals
and
other
structures
in river valley
projects.<<PARAGRAPH_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>0.3
In reporting
the results
of measurement
made
in according
with
this standard,
if the final value,
observed
or calculated,
is to be rounded
off, it shall be done in accordance
with IS  :  2-1960*.<<PARAGRAPH_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>1. SCOPE<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>1.1  This standard
covers the method
of measurement
of lining of canals
and other structures
in river valley
projects
works.<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>2.  GENERAL
RULES<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>2.1 Clubbing
of Items
-
Items
may
be clubbed
together
provided
these are on the basis of the detailed
description
of items
stated
in this
standard.<<HEADING_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>2.2  Booking
of Dimensions
-
In booking
dimensions,
the order shall
be consistent
and generally
in the sequence
of length,
breadth
or width
and height
or depth
or thickness.

for rounding

<<PARAGRAPH>>2.3 Description
of Items
-The
description
of each item shall, unless
stated
otherwise,
be had
to include
where
necessary,
conveyance
and
delivery,
handling,
loading,
unloading,
storing,
hoisting,
lowering,
all
labour
for finishing
to required
shape and size.<<PARAGRAPH_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>2.4  Dimensions
-
All works shall be measured
net in decimal
system
as fixed in its place
as given in 2.4.1
and 2.4.2.<<HEADING_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>2.4.1
Dimensions
shall
be
measured
to
the nearest
0’01
m except
membrane
which
shall be measured
to the nearest
0’001 m.<<PARAGRAPH_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>2.4.2
Areas shall be worked
out to the nearest
0’01 m2.<<PARAGRAPH_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>2.5 Work
to be
Measured
Separately-
Work
executed
in
the
following
conditions
shall be measured
separately:<<HEADING_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>a) Work
in normal
dry condition,
that is, the condition
in which
lining
can be laid without
dewatering;<<PARAGRAPH_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>b) Work
under
water;<<PARAGRAPH_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>c) Work
in liquid
mud;
and<<PARAGRAPH_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>d) Work
interrupted
by tides.<<PARAGRAPH_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>2.5.1
The
dewartering
to
be
done
in
items
(b)
to
(dj
shall
be
measured
separately
as in IS : 4901
( Part 2 )-1982*.<<PARAGRAPH_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>2.6  Bill of Quantities
-
The bills of quantities
shall fully describe
the
materials
and workmanship,
and
accurately
represent
the work
to be
executed.<<HEADING_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>2.7
The work in beds and sides shall be measured
separately.<<PARAGRAPH_END>>

OF MEASUREMENT<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>3.1  The brick/tile/stone/rubble/concrete/membrane
lining

and include

applicable
):<<HEADING_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>a) Preparation
of sub-grade
to

level

and fine dressing
according
to detailed
specifications;<<PARAGRAPH_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>b) Removal
of waste;
and<<PARAGRAPH_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>c) Laying
of
lining
according
to
detailed
specifications
and
curing.<<PARAGRAPH_END>>

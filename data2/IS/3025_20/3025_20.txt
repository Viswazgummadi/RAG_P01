<<HEADING level=3>>1. scope
-
Prescribes
dye dispersion
method
for determination
of
dispersion
characteristics
of a
water body.<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>2. Principle
-
Dispersion
characteristics
of a water body are determined
using
the organic
pigment
*hodamine-B.<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>3. Apparatus
-
Fluorometer.<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>4. Reagents<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>4.1  Sodium Chloride -
saturated
solution.<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>4.2  Dye-
Rhodamine-B
dissolved
in methanol in the proportion
1  :  5 resulting
in a solution
of density
0’8 g/ml approximately.<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>5. Procedure<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>5.1 Increase the density of rhodamine-B
solution,
by adding
saturated
sodium
chloride
solution,
to
the value of the density of the water body.
Inject this solution
into the water body.<<HEADING_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>5.2 At
regular
intervals,
draw
a sample
from the
centre of the patch (  r = 0  )  and determine
the
concentration
of rhodamine-B
using a fluorometer,
which measures the fluorescence
of the dye present
in the sample.
Hence obtain the concentration
of the dye.<<PARAGRAPH_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>5.2 frecautions-The
following
should be noted before the results are interpreted:<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>a) Fluorescence of rhodamine-B
decreases by about 2 percent  per  “C  increase
in temperature;<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>b) Effect of the chlorinity
of water is insignificant;<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>c) The dye is heavily adsorbed by organic suspended
matter and this
adsorption
decreases
with
increasing chlorinity;<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>d) In bright
sunlight
the
fluorescence
decreases
by
about
2
percent
per. hour
and
by
about 0’5 percent per hour in cloudy
conditions:<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>e) It may be noted that the position of the centre of the patch of the dye is an indicator
of the
movement of the water body; and<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>f) The dispersion
can also
be measured
directly
by using
an  in-situ
fluorometer,
which is
commercially
available.<<HEADING_END>>

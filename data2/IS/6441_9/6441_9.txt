<<PARAGRAPH>>0.1  This Indian Standard
( Part IX ) was adopted by the Indian

<<PARAGRAPH>>0.2
Autoclaved
cellular
concrete
is a class of material,
which
has been
developed
commercially
abroad
and is in the process of development
in this
country
also.
A series of Indian
Standards
on cellular
concrete
is being
formulated
so as to provide
guidance
in obtaining
reliable
products
in
autoclaved
cellular
concrete.
The Sectional
Committee
has considered
it
desirable
to issue a standard
for the methods of tests for autoclaved
cellular
concrete
products
for the guidance
of manufacturers
and users.<<PARAGRAPH_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>0.3
In the formulation
of this standard
due weightage
has been given to
international
co-ordination
among the standards
and practices
prevailing
in
different
countries in addition to relating it to the practices in the field in this
country.<<PARAGRAPH_END>>

<<HEADING level=3>>1. SCOPE<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>1.1  This standard
( Part IX ) covers the procedure
for testing the jointing
of autoclaved
cellular concrete flexural members,
such as, floor and roof slabs.<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>2.  TEST SPECIMENS<<HEADING_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>2.1 Size of Specimens -
For determining
the strength
of joints
a series,
consisting
of 3 specimens
shall be tested.
Each
specimen
shall
consist
of
S element parts conforming
in all respects to the requirements
of the relevant
Indian
Standard
( or the requirements
specified
by the
manufacturer
)
in respect of shape and dimensions,
but of length 0.5 m ( see Fig. 1 ) jointed
together.<<PARAGRAPH_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>2.1.1
Temperature
of Specimen -
The
temperature
of the specimen
shall
not be materially
different
from the ambient
temperature
in which
it is
being tested and in any case not less than 0°C.<<PARAGRAPH_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>2.1.2
Moisture Content of Specimen -
The moisture
content
of the concrete
during
the test should be indicated
and should be not less than 10 perceht
by weight
when
determined
in
accordance
with
IS  :  6441
1972t.
( Part 1 ) -<<PARAGRAPH_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>2.2 Jointing-The
jointing
shall be done in accordance
with the direc-
tions of the manufacturer.
If the manufacturer
has prescribed
a modified
method
of jointings
for use in cold weather,
the specimens
jointed
in that
way shall also be tested in accordance
with this procedure.<<PARAGRAPH_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>2.2.1
When
no jointing
material
is required
for interaction
of  loading
between
the
adjacent
elements,
the jointing
of specimens
shall be done
without
the jointing
material
in
accordance
with the directions
of the
manufacturer.<<PARAGRAPH_END>>

The loading shall be applied with a linear load,

The

of f 1.5 percent.<<PARAGRAPH_END>>

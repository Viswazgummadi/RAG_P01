<<PARAGRAPH>>This part of ISO 3506 specifies the mechanical properties of bolts, screws and  studs
made
of austenitic,
martensitic
and ferritic
grades
of corrosion-resistant
stainless
steels
when
tested
over
an ambient
temperature
range
of 15 “C to
25 “C.  Propetiies will vary at higher or lower temperatures.<<PARAGRAPH_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>It applies
to bolts,
screws
and studs<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>—
with nominal
thread
diameter
(d) up to and including
39 mm;<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>—
of triangular
ISO metric
threads
with
diameters
and pitches
in accordance
with
ISO 68-1,
ISO 261 and ISO 262;<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>—
of any shape.<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>It does not apply
to screws
with
special
properties
such as weldability.<<HEADING_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>This part of ISO 3506
does
not define
corrosion
or oxidation
resistance
in particular
environments,
however
some
information
on
materials
for
particular
environments
is given
in anr,ex
E. Regarding
definitions
of corrosion
and
corrosion
resistance
see ISO 8044.<<PARAGRAPH_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>The
aim
of this
part
of
ISO 3506
is a classification
into
property
classes
of
corrosion
resistant
stainless
steel<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>fasteners.
Some
materials
can be used at temperatures
down
to – 200 “C, some
can
be used
at temperatures
up to<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>+ 800 “C in air. Information
on the influence
of temperature
on mechanical
properties
is found
in annex
F.<<HEADING_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>Corrosion
and oxidation
performances
and mechanical
propetiies
for use at elevated
or sub-zero
temperatures
must
be the subject
of agreement
between
user and manufacturer
in each
particular
case.
Annex
G shows
how the
risk of
intergranular
corrosion
at elevated
temperatures.
depends
on the carbon
content.<<PARAGRAPH_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>All austenitic

steel fasteners

H).<<HEADING_END>>

<<HEADING level=3>>1  Scope<<HEADING_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>This part of ISO  3506
specifies the mechanical
properties of set screws and similar fasteners
not under tensile stress
made of austenitic
stainless steel when tested over an ambient
temperature
range of 15  “C  to  25 ‘C.  Properties
will
vary at higher or lower temp~ratures.<<PARAGRAPH_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>It applies to set screws and similar fasteners<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>—
with nominal thread diameters
(d) from 1,6 mm up to and including  24  mm;<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>—
of triangular
ISO metric threads with diameters
and pitches according to ISO 68-1, ISO 261 and ISO 262;<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>—
of any shape.<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>It does not apply to screws with special
properties such as weldability.<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>This part of ISO 3506 does not define corrosion or oxidation
resistance in particular environments.<<HEADING_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>The
aim
of this
part of ISO 3506
is a classification
into property
classes
of corrosion
resistant
stainless
steel
fasteners.
Corrosion
and
oxidation
performances
and
mechanical
properties
for
use
at elevated
or
sub-zero
temperatures
must be the subject of agreement
between
user and
manufacturer
in each particular
case. Annex D
shows how the risk of intergranular
corrosion at elevated temperatures
depends on the carbon content.<<PARAGRAPH_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>All
austenitic
stainless
steel fasteners
are normally
non-magnetic
in the annealed
condition;
after cold working,
some magnetic
properties
may be evident (see annex E).<<HEADING_END>>

<<HEADING level=3>>A.2 Steel group A (austenitic
structure)<<HEADING_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>Five main grades of austenitic steels, Al to A5, are included in ISO 3506-1,
ISO 3506-2 and ISO 3506-3. They cannot
be hardened
and are usually non-magnetic.
In order to reduce the susceptibility
to work hardening
copper may be
added to the steel grades Al to A5 as specified in table 2.<<PARAGRAPH_END>>

applies.<<HEADING_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>As chromic
oxide
mekes steel resistant to corrosion,
low carbon content
is of great importance
to non-stabilized
steels. Due to the high affinity of chrome to carbon, chrome
carbide is obtained
instead of chromic
oxide which
is
more likely at elevated temperature.
(See annex D.)<<PARAGRAPH_END>>

applies.<<HEADING_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>The elements
Ti, Nb or Ta affect the carbon and chromic oxide is produced to its full extent.<<HEADING_END>>

or similar

<<HEADING level=3>>A.2. 1 Steel grade Al<<HEADING_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>Steel grade Al
is especially
designed
for machining.
Due to the high sulfur content of the steels within this grade
have lower resistance to corrosion than corresponding
steels with normal sulfur content.<<PARAGRAPH_END>>
<<HEADING level=3>>A.2.2 Steel grade A2<<HEADING_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>Steels of grade A2 are the most frequently
used stainless steels. They are used for kitchen equipment
and apparatus

Steels within
this grade are not suitable

acid and agents with

<<HEADING level=3>>A.2.4 Steel grade A4<<HEADING_END>>
<<PARAGRAPH>>Steels of grade “A4are “acid proof steels”, which are Mo alloyed and give considerably
better resistance to corrosion.
A4 is used to a great extent by the cellulose
